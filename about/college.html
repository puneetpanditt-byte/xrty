Our History

Maitreyi College, a constituent college of the University of Delhi, was founded in 1967 by the Delhi Administration. Named after the renowned woman scholar Maitreyi, the wife of the great sage Yajnavalkya, the college embodies her spirit of learning and intellectual pursuit.

The college began its journey with a modest enrollment of 217 students in the school building of Sarvodaya Kanya Vidyalaya in C-Block, Lajpat Nagar. The founding Principal, Late Dr. C.S. Sharma, along with a dedicated team of teachers, laid the foundation of what would become one of the most prestigious women's colleges in the country.

In 1970, the college shifted to its present location in Chanakya Puri, New Delhi. The new building, designed by renowned architect Mr. J.K. Chowdhury, provided ample space for the growing institution. Over the decades, the college has expanded its infrastructure, added new courses, and established itself as a center of academic excellence.
